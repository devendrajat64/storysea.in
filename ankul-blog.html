The Unconstructed Road to Paris - Ankul
Ankul Prajapati
JULY 4, 2020
Inspirational

अपना भी एक सफर रहा है, “The unconstructed road to Paris”. Hi, I am Ankul Prajapati, a physics aspirant and 2020 Mech Eng. graduate SVNIT, from a small town in Shahdol District, Madhya Pradesh, where, electricity came after completing my 11th. Throughout school days, I used the kerosene lamp for late-night study and read the newspaper for seizing the new updates at local shops or mills. The situation was not always grim here. I also enjoyed my childhood with nature. I still remembered when I was reading the newspaper, saw an advertisement of Resonance Cota coaching classes written “IIT-Chhoo-Lo-Apni-Zindagi”, at that time I was in class 10th. Hopefully same time I came to know about NTSE and qualified. Later on, I searched about IIT, NIT and JEE etc and started preparing for it by self. The 11th and 12th time were full of transformation and changes (Long story) still I managed to score well in JEE Mains and Board Exam (District 2nd Rank). After completing school, I always wanted to pursue pure physics and become a theoretical physicist but my own put a condition of the institutes, If I get IITs, IISc or IISER then only will choose physics for undergraduate otherwise will go for engineering and then complete higher study in physics. Unfortunately, I couldn't able to score well in JEE Advance and finally decided to choose Mechanical Engineering at NIT Surat. (I won’t write any college life story, which is as usual and I also enjoyed a lot) Till first year I was not aware that, in future engineering degree would not be admitted at most of the institution for physics master or PhD. When I got to know all the scenario, somehow, I able to survive and started hunting for opportunities to the student like me. In the 1st and 2nd year of BTech spent more than 50% time sitting in front of a laptop and doing research and finding my interest.

After discerning my interest in astrophysics and cosmology worked on a few projects. After spending the whole one year understood that it's not my cup of tea because of lots of data analysis work involved in astrophysics. Later on, I explored my second area of interest that is Quantum Technology and sustaining the same. At the edge of the first year, I conceived let's see if some student chapter can help me in strengthening the profile but found worthless. This was the time when I believed to found a research organisation dedicated to undergraduate research work and finally with 4 other students founded a research group named Bose.X, a mission to prepare multiple S N Bose or CV Raman like a scientist for India (https://www.bosex.org/about-us) and currently, it is consisting of 25 core-noncore members.

Time was dragging very fast and till second year I didn’t have any clear path on what to do. Luckily, in the second year of course, during winter, I received an acceptance for a course by Indian Academy of Science where I joined many renowned scientists including Royal Society of London fellows. And this was the turning point as few of them advised to write research papers to get selected at the best physics institute and the day onwards, I started working my own with other junior friends under Bose.X group. In the second year again could not get a physics internship and joined at Cryogenic Engineering Centre IIT KGP, where I had the opportunity to work on semi cryogenic rocket engine, the obtained results have been already published in reputed Journal and International conferences. The same result presented at an ISRO conference and this was my first conference. I believe in practical of life and self-motivation. In this conference there were no other undergraduate students, this got me a very high thrust of motivation which was kindled after meeting with Missile Woman Dr Tessy Thomas and Brahmos CEO Dr Mishra. During the third year, I continued in quantum technology and worked on the quantum dot-based photovoltaics and presented a few papers in international conferences. Also selected for UNESCO funded conference held at International Centre for Theoretical Physics Italy. After the third year, I was expecting to get an intern abroad but due to funding issue could not. So, decided to be in India and offered for three internships (IUCAA, IISc and BARC). According to the project and area of interest, I decided to go for IUCAA where I worked with Dr Joydeep Bagchi (Lead discoverer of farthest known supercluster Saraswati). For the last three year of the BTech, I sweated in many research projects and published the obtained theoretical and computational results at a total of eight reputed National-International Conferences and journals including “Springer Nature”. Most of the work completed autonomously.

Now the time came when I have to decide whether to go for direct PhD or Master. As I was not thoroughly ready for writing the Physics GRE (There are two separate papers 1st General and 2nd Subjective, PCM) so I left with only European and Australian universities. Before applying for the next degree, I was owning a total of 8 research papers (5 Conf + 3 Journals) so I was quite confident about the selection. And finally, till date got the offer from ANU Australia (QS W Ranking 31), NUS Singapore (QS W Ranking 11), Sorbonne University (Physics Research USN World Ranking 17) & Ecole Polytechnique France, Karlsruhe Institute of Technology Germany, KTH Royal Sweden and the Sapienza University of Rome in Physics. Some of having full funding also. Most probably I will be joining Ecole or Sorbonne in Paris, France. This was all about the Turbulences of the shift from Engineering to Physics and now onwards no more Mechanical guess what next?



When you work on some unprecedented path, it’s obvious you can’t get instant results, you have to be in same for a long period, to do it successfully self-motivation is the only sign. Believe me, nowhere extra talent is expected, just you have to invest your time with allegiance, the result will defiantly come. So, build your well-framed path by connecting arbitrary dots and let everyone know.
